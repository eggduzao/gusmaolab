Title: Writing
Slug: writing
Date: 2025-11-10
Summary: Technical essays, notes, and literary experiments on data engineering, bioinformatics, infrastructure, and the strange machinery of modern work.

Writing is where I turn technical work into something slower, clearer, and more durable.

Some pieces here come from my ongoing technical book, **Data Engineer in Bioinformatics**, where I write about pipelines, data modeling, validation, distributed systems, and the uncomfortable reality that scientific data is usually messier than the diagrams suggest.

Other pieces are essays, notes, and fragments about data engineering, software practice, reproducibility, and the craft of building systems that other people can trust. I am especially interested in the border between **data platforms**, **machine learning**, **healthcare data**, and **bioinformatics** — not as separate fields, but as parts of the same practical problem: turning complex real-world information into reliable structures.

This page also includes literary work, especially excerpts from **Mikrokosmos I**, my ongoing fiction project. That side is less about clean architecture and more about recursion, absurdity, memory, language, and small fictional machines that behave badly.

In short: some texts here are technical, some are literary, and some are both. The common thread is structure — how we build it, how it breaks, and what remains interesting when it does.
